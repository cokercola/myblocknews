About myblocknews

Last updated July 2026

I still remember the first time I bought crypto. I had no idea what a private key was, I didn't understand gas fees, and I definitely didn't understand why my "investment" dropped 30% overnight while everyone online acted like that was normal. I spent months piecing together information from a dozen different sources — some good, most confusing, a few outright scams dressed up as advice.

That gap is why myblocknews exists.

I love this space. Not in a hype, get-rich-quick way, but because I think blockchain technology is genuinely one of the more interesting shifts happening in finance and technology right now. I love following the projects, the debates, the technical breakthroughs, and yes, even the drama. But I don't love how hard it is for a newcomer to find honest, clear information without wading through jargon or getting sold something.

What I'm trying to build

My goal with myblocknews is simple: help people actually understand crypto — not just the price charts, but how it works, why it matters, where the risks are, and how to think critically about a space that moves fast and doesn't always reward the cautious. Whether you're here to learn what a blockchain actually is, keep up with news on the coins you hold, or just get a second opinion before you buy something a group chat is hyping up, I want this to be a place where you leave a little more informed than when you arrived.

What we are

A simple, fast reference point. Live prices for major coins, refreshed automatically, alongside a running feed of headlines pulled from trusted third-party publications, plus original writing on the parts of crypto the marketing doesn't cover.

What we're not

I'm not a financial advisor, and nothing on this site is a recommendation to buy or sell anything. This isn't a brokerage or an exchange — we don't hold funds or execute trades. See our Privacy Policy and Terms for the full picture.

Sourcing

Market data comes from CoinGecko's public API. Headlines are aggregated from outlets including CoinDesk, Cointelegraph, and Decrypt, with attribution shown next to each headline. We link out to original sources rather than reproducing full articles.

Thanks for reading — and welcome to myblocknews.
